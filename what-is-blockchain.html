What is Blockchain?

Understanding the foundational technology behind cryptocurrencies and secure data systems.

Decentralized Digital Ledger

At its core, blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across many computers. This ensures that no single entity has control over the entire network, making it resistant to censorship and single points of failure.

Blocks Linked in a Chain

Data in a blockchain is stored in blocks that are linked together in a chronological chain. Each block contains a timestamp and a reference (hash) to the previous block, creating an unbroken chain of information.

Immutable and Transparent

Once data is recorded in a blockchain, it cannot be altered or deleted without consensus from the network. This immutability, combined with the transparent nature of the ledger, creates a system of trust without requiring a central authority.

Beyond Cryptocurrencies

While blockchain began as the foundation for Bitcoin, its applications now extend far beyond cryptocurrencies. Industries from finance to healthcare, supply chain to voting systems are exploring blockchain's potential for secure and transparent data management.
